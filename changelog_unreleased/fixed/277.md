Closed a destructive-command bypass (audit Theme B, EI of Directive #275): the `rm`/`mv`/`cp` force/recursive out-of-scope block could be evaded by placing the flag **after** the operand — `rm /path/outside-registry -rf` (valid GNU syntax) — because the entry pre-filter required the flag to follow the verb with only other flags between. The pre-filter now recognizes the flag anywhere in the verb's argument run, including after operands, while still **stopping at a pipeline boundary** (`;` `|` `&`) so a flag belonging to another command (`rm ./ok && ls -rf`) does not trigger it (#212 cross-command anchoring preserved), and matching the flag as a whole token so a `-rf` inside a filename is not a false trigger. `check_destructive_args` (the in-scope/out-of-scope decision) was already operand-position-agnostic; only the entry pre-filter needed widening. In-scope permutations (`rm ./build -rf`) remain allowed.
